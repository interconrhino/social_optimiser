**Assistant:** Research complete.

The closest real-world analogue to your problem in the academic and deployment literature is an NGO in India called ARMMAN, which runs a weekly automated voice-advisory service for pregnant women and uses a small budget of personal advisor calls to re-engage mothers who have started drifting away from the voice messages. Structurally that is your problem in a different domain — a population of tens of thousands, a small call budget, a cumulative-engagement objective, historical data gathered under a non-random allocation rule, and equity constraints on who gets served. They use a method called a **restless multi-armed bandit with a Whittle-index policy**, fit using a technique called **decision-focused learning** to handle the fact that their historical call data was not randomised, with a fairness modification (the literature calls it "ProbFair") to guarantee a minimum per-subgroup call rate. Their deployment has RCT-grade evidence of improving engagement over a rotation rule.

In plain terms, this family computes a "call-priority score" for each farmer every month from the learned model, and you pick the top 400. The fairness modification guarantees a per-subgroup floor before the top-400 step, and decision-focused learning is the trick that keeps the confounded historical data from pointing the algorithm at the wrong target.

The best specific method for your situation is: **a Whittle-index policy on a partially observable restless bandit model of your farmers, with transition parameters fit via decision-focused learning, and a ProbFair-style minimum-pull-probability floor per (village × language) subgroup.** In plain terms: a monthly priority score per farmer that reflects how much future engagement we expect to gain by calling them now, a guaranteed minimum call share for every subgroup, a cooldown so farmers aren't called two months running, and a plain-English reason attached to each farmer on the 400-list. **Assistant:** Here is the final recommendation.

**Technique:** Restless Multi-Armed Bandit (RMAB) with a Whittle-index policy.
**Algorithm:** Whittle-index on a Collapsing / Partially-Observable RMAB, parameters fit via decision-focused learning, with a ProbFair equity floor per (village × language) subgroup.

**What you'll do each month:** For each of your ~28,000 farmers, compute a per-farmer priority score from the learned engagement-state model. Enforce a minimum call share for every subgroup first, then fill up to 400 with the highest-scoring remaining farmers, respecting a one-month cooldown. Output the list with a plain-English rationale per selection, plus a fairness dashboard.

**How you'll know it works:** The board-approved side-by-side pilot — stratified randomisation into treatment (this method) vs. control (current rotation), both arms sharing the 400-call budget proportionally, 6–8 months, difference-in-differences at the end with clustered bootstrap for the confidence interval. Pre-committed equity-breach stopping rule.

**What to expect:** In the closest-analogue deployment (ARMMAN, maternal mHealth in India, structurally the same problem), this method family delivered RCT-validated improvements in engagement over a rotation baseline. GreenFurrow's realised lift is what the pilot is designed to measure.
